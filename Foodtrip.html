Foodtrip

Sutukil

In Siquijor, Sutukil is a beloved culinary tradition where diners handpick fresh seafood and opt for grilling (sugba), soup-based dishes (tula), or raw marinated delicacies (kilaw). This dining experience celebrates the island's bounty of seafood while offering a variety of flavorful options.

Pan Bisaya

In Siquijor, "Pan Bisaya" refers to a local version of Filipino bread, known for its soft, fluffy texture and slightly sweet taste. It's a popular breakfast or snack option, often enjoyed with coffee or hot chocolate, showcasing the island's culinary delights.

Peanut Balls

In Siquijor, peanut balls are a beloved local delicacy. They're typically made by blending roasted peanuts with sugar or molasses to form a sticky paste. This mixture is then shaped into small balls and may be coated with sesame seeds or grated coconut. Peanut balls are a popular snack in Siquijor, offering a delicious combination of sweetness and nuttiness that locals and visitors enjoy.

Peanut Brittle

Peanut brittle in Siquijor is a crunchy and sweet treat made by caramelizing sugar with peanuts. It's a beloved local snack that showcases the island's flavors and culinary traditions.

Otap

Otap in Siquijor is a crisp, flaky pastry layered with butter, sugar, and sesame seeds, offering a delightful local twist on this beloved Filipino treat.

Torta

In Siquijor, "torta" is a local cake or bread made with flour, eggs, sugar, and often coconut. It's dense, moist, and may feature flavors like jackfruit or ube, offering a delicious taste of the island's culinary heritage.
